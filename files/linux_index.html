Linux Mint 'Cinnamon'

My top reccomended distro for everyone

Linux Mint is an amazing distro that I can reccomend to anyone, especially if you are swapping from Windows. Mint will give you what everyone likes about windows, while being much more secure. For the most part you can live with peace of mind that while browsing the web, or downloading files, you will most likely be just fine. Linux Mint, even on the heavier 'Cinnamon' desktop, is still far lighter than Windows 11. Coming from Windows Linux Mint will feel like home, while not sending your data to Microsoft, or shoving AI down your throat.

Other Versions

Linux Mint comes in 2 other versions, MATE & XFCE

Both MATE and XFCE are pretty lightweight, and are used as lightweight alternatives to the heavier Cinnamon desktop, Cinnamon generally will use about 2.2 Gigabytes of ram on idle, whereas XFCE likes to idle at or below 1 Gigabyte of ram, and MATE will idle roughly between those two.

XorinOS 'Pro'

Very easy, hard to mess up

XorinOS is backed by a company, and offers a paid version of their Distro, and while this does raise some questions about what Linux is "supposed to be," it does come with a unique advantage: They HAVE to make sure it works, and they HAVE to stay competive to sell in an environment that is well known for being free. Other than its stability, XorinOS has some really neat features I wish were common in other distros. XorinOS has, built into its themes, options to make your system look like Windows (The default), MacOS, or many others that may be more well suited to your liking!

Other Versions

XorinOS comes in 3 major versions: Pro, Core, & Education

XorinOS pro, as covered above, is their flagship version, with all of their features, but is't free.
Core comes with most of what Pro offers, with a few non-essentials locked away, including the MacOS theme and a few others.
Education is a special tailored version designed to be used by Schools & Students, but for the purposes of this guide, just ignore this version.

Q4OS 'Trinity'

Extremely lightweight, some knowhow required

Q4OS is honestly such a treat to get to use, idling around 400-500 Megabytes of ram usage. It almost has a Windows 7 feel to it
